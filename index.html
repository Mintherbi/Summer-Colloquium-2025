Historical Lineage

The study of neural networks traces back to the foundational work on C. elegans, the first organism to have its complete connectome mapped. This 302-neuron nervous system serves as a crucial reference point for understanding how biological neural networks process information and make decisions.

C. elegans Connectome

Understanding the connectome enables us to control behavior and decision-making processes in biological entities.

Challenging Human Free Will

Scientific research challenging human free will has been conducted for many years already. Choice prediction research demonstrates our ability to anticipate decisions before conscious awareness.

Historical Lineage
Hacking Decision Making and Behavior

Through precise manipulation of C. elegans neural circuits, researchers have demonstrated the ability to control fundamental behaviors and decision-making processes. This groundbreaking work reveals how specific neural pathways govern choice, movement, and survival responses, providing a roadmap for understanding more complex nervous systems.

The implications extend far beyond simple organisms. As we decode the neural basis of decision-making, we gain unprecedented insights into the mechanisms that drive behavior across all species, including humans. This research forms the foundation for both therapeutic interventions and raises profound questions about the nature of free will and autonomous choice.

In Silico

The transition from studying brains in living organisms (in vivo) and laboratory dishes (in vitro) to computer simulations (in silico) represents a fundamental shift in neuroscience methodology. This revolutionary approach enables researchers to explore questions about consciousness and free will that were previously impossible to investigate experimentally.

Digital Brain Revolution

Computational simulation becomes a primary research tool alongside traditional experimental approaches. The ability to create digital replicas of neural circuits, test hypotheses in silico, and simulate brain functions at multiple scales opens unprecedented possibilities for understanding consciousness, decision-making, and the very nature of mind.

Virtual Neuroscience

Major international research initiatives demonstrate the global commitment to this ambitious goal of creating comprehensive digital brain models that can advance our understanding of neural mechanisms and potentially unlock the secrets of human cognition.

Human Brain Project (HBP)

A European flagship project (2013-2023) that pioneered digital neuroscience, creating computational models and simulations of brain circuits and regions.

Blue Brain Project

Led by EPFL, this Swiss initiative developed algorithms to build biologically faithful digital copies of brain circuits, including detailed neuron morphologies and connectomes.

EBRAINS

An open research infrastructure providing data, tools, and computing facilities for brain-related research, enabling researchers worldwide to build and simulate neural networks.

Open Brain Institute

A foundation supporting the global community in utilizing simulation neuroscience to study the brain and its diseases through virtual neuroscience labs.

My Vision: Accessible Brain Structure Learning

What I want to create is a platform that enables people to easily understand brain structure through interactive exploration. The challenge is finding the sweet spot between scientific accuracy and public accessibility in neuroscience education.

The Current Landscape

Scientific platforms are accurate but require high computational power and are burdensome for general understanding. Gaming platforms like Spore allow direct body manipulation and survival strategy thinking, but lack educational depth about neural mechanisms.

My Educational Platform Goal

I aim to create an educational platform that combines the engagement of games with the educational value of scientific simulation. This platform would democratize neuroscience education, making complex brain structures and neural processes understandable and engaging for students, educators, and curious minds without requiring extensive technical background or computational resources.

Bridging the Gap

By leveraging interactive visualization and simplified yet accurate models, this platform will make the complexity of neural networks accessible to a broader audience, fostering a new generation of neuroscience enthusiasts and informed citizens in our digital age.

1. LIF Neuron & Spiking Neural Network (SNN)
Computationally Efficient Neural Modeling

The simulation starts with computationally efficient Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) neurons. These simplified yet biologically plausible models capture the essential dynamics of neural membrane potential, providing the perfect balance between computational efficiency and biological realism for interactive exploration.

Spiking Neural Networks

LIF neurons are assembled into Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), where neurons communicate through discrete, timed spikes rather than continuous signals. This approach captures the temporal dynamics of real neural processing, revealing how timing and synchronization drive complex neural computations and emergent behaviors.

Temporal Dynamics

Unlike traditional artificial neural networks, SNNs preserve the crucial temporal dimension of neural activity. This enables the simulation of realistic neural phenomena such as spike-timing dependent plasticity, oscillations, and the precise timing codes that underlie biological information processing.

2. Analysis in Latent Space
Dimensionality Reduction

To make the complex, high-dimensional neural firing patterns intuitive, we use advanced dimensionality reduction techniques such as PCA, t-SNE, and UMAP. These methods reveal hidden patterns and structures within the neural activity that would be impossible to perceive in the original high-dimensional space.

Latent Space Visualization

Neural activity is translated into a lower-dimensional 'latent space' - a compressed representation that preserves the essential dynamics while making them visually accessible. This approach enables real-time exploration of how neural patterns evolve, cluster, and transition during different behavioral states and computational processes.

Pattern Recognition

The latent space visualization reveals the underlying structure and dynamics of the network, allowing users to identify recurring patterns, state transitions, and emergent behaviors. This provides crucial insights into how distributed neural activity gives rise to coordinated computation and behavior.

3. Neural Connection Visualization

Finally, the structural connections of the neural network (the connectome) are visualized. This interactive graph allows for exploring the intricate web of connections, filtering by neuron type, and understanding the static architecture that gives rise to the dynamic activity.
