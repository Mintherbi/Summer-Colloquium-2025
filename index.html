Historical Lineage

The study of neural networks traces back to the foundational work on C. elegans, the first organism to have its complete connectome mapped. This 302-neuron nervous system serves as a crucial reference point for understanding how biological neural networks process information and make decisions.

C. elegans Connectome

Understanding the connectome enables us to control behavior and decision-making processes in biological entities.

Challenging Human Free Will

Scientific research challenging human free will has been conducted for many years already. Choice prediction research demonstrates our ability to anticipate decisions before conscious awareness.

Historical Lineage
Hacking Decision Making and Behavior

Through precise manipulation of C. elegans neural circuits, researchers have demonstrated the ability to control fundamental behaviors and decision-making processes. This groundbreaking work reveals how specific neural pathways govern choice, movement, and survival responses, providing a roadmap for understanding more complex nervous systems.

The implications extend far beyond simple organisms. As we decode the neural basis of decision-making, we gain unprecedented insights into the mechanisms that drive behavior across all species, including humans. This research forms the foundation for both therapeutic interventions and raises profound questions about the nature of free will and autonomous choice.

In Silico

The transition from studying brains in living organisms (in vivo) and laboratory dishes (in vitro) to computer simulations (in silico) represents a fundamental shift in neuroscience methodology. This revolutionary approach enables researchers to explore questions about consciousness and free will that were previously impossible to investigate experimentally.

Digital Brain Revolution

Computational simulation becomes a primary research tool alongside traditional experimental approaches. The ability to create digital replicas of neural circuits, test hypotheses in silico, and simulate brain functions at multiple scales opens unprecedented possibilities for understanding consciousness, decision-making, and the very nature of mind.

Virtual Neuroscience

Major international research initiatives demonstrate the global commitment to this ambitious goal of creating comprehensive digital brain models that can advance our understanding of neural mechanisms and potentially unlock the secrets of human cognition.

Human Brain Project (HBP)

A European flagship project (2013-2023) that pioneered digital neuroscience, creating computational models and simulations of brain circuits and regions.

Blue Brain Project

Led by EPFL, this Swiss initiative developed algorithms to build biologically faithful digital copies of brain circuits, including detailed neuron morphologies and connectomes.

EBRAINS

An open research infrastructure providing data, tools, and computing facilities for brain-related research, enabling researchers worldwide to build and simulate neural networks.

Open Brain Institute

A foundation supporting the global community in utilizing simulation neuroscience to study the brain and its diseases through virtual neuroscience labs.

Neurosecurity: Protecting Free Will in the Digital Age

As our understanding of neural mechanisms deepens and brain-computer interfaces become more sophisticated, a new field has emerged to address the critical need for protecting human cognitive autonomy. Neurosecurity represents the intersection of neuroscience, cybersecurity, and ethics, focusing on safeguarding the most fundamental aspects of human identity and decision-making.

"The protection of the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of neural devices from malicious parties with the goal of preserving the safety of a person's neural mechanisms, neural computation, and free will"
— Ienca, M., & Haselager, P. (2016). Hacking the brain: brain-computer interfacing technology and the ethics of neurosecurity. Neurosurgical Focus, 40(5), E13.[3]

This definition encapsulates the urgent need to develop protective measures as neural technologies advance and become more integrated into daily life.

The historical trajectory from early connectome mapping through advanced brain simulation to predictive decision algorithms culminates in this critical question: How do we preserve human agency in an age where thoughts and decisions can be monitored, predicted, and potentially influenced? Neurosecurity emerges not just as a technical challenge, but as a fundamental requirement for maintaining human dignity and autonomy in the digital age.

My Community of Practice

My vision is to create a platform that makes brain structure accessible to everyone—bridging the gap between complex scientific simulations and engaging educational experiences. Currently, the landscape is divided between highly accurate but resource-intensive scientific tools and entertaining but educationally limited games.

Scientific Simulation Platforms

Scientific simulation platforms offer precise neural modeling but require significant computational resources and expertise:

NEST Simulator

A highly accurate neural network simulation tool that models spiking neural networks with biological precision, but demands substantial computational power and technical knowledge.

OpenWorm

An ambitious project creating a comprehensive digital simulation of C. elegans, providing scientifically accurate modeling but complex for general public understanding.

Gaming and Interactive Platforms

Gaming platforms prioritize engagement and accessibility but often lack educational depth:

Spore

A evolution simulation game that allows players to directly manipulate creature bodies and experience natural survival strategies. While fun and engaging, it has limitations as an educational tool for understanding neural mechanisms.

Hybrid Platforms

Some platforms attempt to bridge scientific accuracy with accessibility:

MuJoCo Playground

A physics simulation environment that balances computational efficiency with realistic modeling, making complex simulations more accessible to researchers and developers.

AnimatLab

A neuromechanical simulation tool that combines neural network modeling with biomechanical simulation, offering a middle ground between complexity and usability.

My Vision: Accessible Brain Structure Learning

What I want to create is a platform that enables people to easily understand brain structure through interactive exploration. The challenge is finding the sweet spot between scientific accuracy and public accessibility:

  • Scientific platforms are accurate but require high computational power and are burdensome for general understanding
  • Gaming platforms like Spore allow direct body manipulation and survival strategy thinking, but lack educational depth
  • My goal is to create an educational platform that combines the engagement of games with the educational value of scientific simulation

This platform would democratize neuroscience education, making complex brain structures and neural processes understandable and engaging for students, educators, and curious minds without requiring extensive technical background or computational resources.

Methods

My methodological approach combines my interdisciplinary background in architecture, computational neuroscience, and computer security to create an accessible brain simulation platform. This unique perspective allows me to bridge the gap between complex neural modeling and intuitive visual design.

Interdisciplinary Foundation

My research methodology draws from three key domains:

  • Architecture - Spatial reasoning and 3D visualization skills for creating intuitive brain structure representations
  • Computational Neuroscience - Understanding of neural mechanisms and mathematical modeling of brain functions
  • Computer Security - Awareness of digital vulnerabilities and the importance of protecting neural data and cognitive autonomy

This interdisciplinary foundation enables me to approach brain simulation from both technical and design perspectives, ensuring both scientific accuracy and user accessibility.

Technical Implementation: Rhino C# Platform

I plan to implement the brain simulation platform using Rhino C# (Grasshopper), leveraging its powerful 3D modeling and computational design capabilities:

  • 3D Visualization - Create interactive 3D representations of neural networks and brain structures
  • Real-time Simulation - Implement dynamic neural activity visualization with immediate feedback
  • User Interaction - Enable direct manipulation of neural parameters and network topology
  • Educational Interface - Design intuitive controls that make complex neuroscience accessible to general users
Theoretical Framework: LIF Neurons and Spiking Neural Networks

The simulation will be based on established neuroscience theory, primarily drawing from Peter Dayan and Larry Abbott's "Theoretical Neuroscience":

Foundational theoretical framework from Dayan & Abbott's "Theoretical Neuroscience"

Leaky Integrate-and-Fire (LIF) Neurons

The foundation of the simulation uses LIF neuron models, which provide a balance between biological realism and computational efficiency. These models capture essential neural dynamics while remaining computationally tractable for real-time interaction.

LIF neuron model showing membrane potential dynamics and channel behavior

Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs)

Rather than abstract neural network representations, the platform will implement spiking neural networks that model the temporal dynamics of neural communication. This approach provides users with a more authentic understanding of how neurons actually communicate through discrete spikes.

Spiking neural network architecture showing temporal spike patterns

Detailed visualization of neural spike timing and propagation

Dynamic Visualization: Latent Space Representation

To make neural dynamics more intuitive, I will represent neural firing patterns in latent space - a lower-dimensional space that captures the essential patterns of neural activity:

Neural firing patterns mapped to lower-dimensional latent space for intuitive understanding

Pedagogical Advantage: By visualizing neural activity in latent space, users can observe how complex patterns of neural firing translate to understandable behavioral outputs, making the connection between neural mechanism and function more apparent.

Morphological Growth Modeling

The platform will incorporate research on neural growth patterns to provide realistic representations of how neurons develop their complex morphologies. Drawing from established computational models:

Neural growth dynamics in response to environmental field constraints

Statistical models capturing probabilistic neural development patterns

"Computational approaches to neural morphology modeling enable us to understand how neurons develop their intricate branching patterns and how these structures relate to function"
— Based on research from PLoS Computational Biology

This morphological modeling will allow users to experiment with different neural growth parameters and observe how structural changes affect network behavior and information processing.

Interactive Experimentation Framework

The ultimate goal is to create a platform where users can conduct their own neural experiments:

  • Parameter Manipulation - Adjust neural properties, connection strengths, and network topology in real-time
  • Visual Feedback - Immediate visualization of how changes affect neural dynamics and network behavior
  • Educational Scenarios - Pre-designed experiments that demonstrate key neuroscience concepts
  • Discovery Learning - Open-ended exploration tools that encourage users to develop their own hypotheses and test them

This methodology combines rigorous scientific modeling with interactive design principles, creating an educational tool that makes neuroscience both accessible and engaging for diverse audiences.
